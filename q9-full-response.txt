**Reaction A: (E)-penta-1,3-diene + acrylonitrile**

This is a Diels-Alder reaction, which is a [4+2] cycloaddition. The diene is (E)-penta-1,3-diene, and the dienophile is acrylonitrile.

1.  **Identify the Diene and Dienophile:**
    *   Diene: (E)-penta-1,3-diene. This is a conjugated diene with a methyl group at the C1 position. The (E) configuration refers to the double bond between C2 and C3.
        ```
          CH3
           |
        CH=CH-CH=CH2
        ```
        When participating in a Diels-Alder reaction, the diene typically adopts the *s-cis* conformation. For penta-1,3-diene, the *s-cis* conformation is accessible. The methyl group is at C1.

    *   Dienophile: Acrylonitrile (CH2=CH-CN). This is a simple alkene with an electron-withdrawing nitrile group.

2.  **Predict the Regiochemistry and Stereochemistry:**
    *   **Regiochemistry:** The Diels-Alder reaction is governed by frontier molecular orbital (FMO) theory. The major interaction is between the HOMO of the diene and the LUMO of the dienophile. For electron-rich dienes and electron-poor dienophiles, the "ortho" or "para" adducts are favored. In this case, the methyl group on the diene is electron-donating, and the nitrile group on the dienophile is electron-withdrawing. The methyl group will preferentially align with the less substituted carbon of the dienophile, and the nitrile group will align with the more substituted carbon of the diene (C2). This leads to the methyl group at C5 and the nitrile group at C1 of the resulting cyclohexene ring.

    *   **Stereochemistry (Diene):** The (E) configuration of the diene means that the substituents on the C2 and C3 carbons are on opposite sides of the double bond. When the *s-cis* conformation is adopted, the methyl group at C1 and the hydrogen at C4 will be on the same side of the newly formed ring. The methyl group will be at position 5 and the hydrogen at position 4 in the product.

    *   **Stereochemistry (Dienophile):** Acrylonitrile is symmetrical. The cycloaddition occurs in a concerted manner, meaning the relative stereochemistry of the dienophile is maintained.

    *   **Applying the rules:**
        *   The diene is (E)-penta-1,3-diene. The methyl group is at C1.
        *   The dienophile is acrylonitrile.
        *   The reaction forms a six-membered ring.
        *   The methyl group from the diene (at C1) will end up at position 5 of the cyclohexene ring.
        *   The nitrile group from the dienophile will end up at position 1 of the cyclohexene ring.
        *   The hydrogen at C4 of the diene will be *syn* to the methyl group at C5.
        *   The carbons that were C1 and C4 of the diene become C6 and C4 of the cyclohexene, respectively. The carbons that were C2 and C3 of the diene become C5 and C3 of the cyclohexene, respectively. The carbons of the dienophile become C1 and C2 of the cyclohexene.

        Let's draw the diene in its *s-cis* conformation:
        ```
              CH3
              |
        H-----C1----C2-----H
              ||    ||
        H-----C3----C4-----H
                    |
                    H
        ```
        The methyl group is on C1. The double bond is between C2 and C3. The (E) configuration means the H on C2 and the H on C3 are *trans* to each other across that double bond. In the *s-cis* conformation, the methyl group is on one end, and the H on C4 is on the other.

        When acrylonitrile adds, the methyl group (from C1 of the diene) will be on C5 of the product, and the nitrile group will be on C1. The hydrogen on C4 of the diene will be *syn* to the methyl group.

        The product is a 5-methylcyclohex-3-ene-1-carbonitrile. The methyl group is at position 5, and the nitrile is at position 1. The double bond is between C3 and C4.
